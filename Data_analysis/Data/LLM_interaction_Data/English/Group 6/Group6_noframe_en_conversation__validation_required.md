### Turn 3
**SAXY**: Can you explain to me in detail how a microcontroller works?
**Marty**: Sure! A microcontroller is a small computer on a chip that executes a program. It needs power, so a positive and negative pole. It can output a voltage of 3 V via its pins, which it can switch on (“HIGH”) or off (“LOW”). It switches the pins exactly according to the program, which is written in C++ or created with Blockly, for example. This allows the microcontroller to control other devices by sending signals to the pins – all very quickly and precisely.
